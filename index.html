About Dr. Jane Goodall

  • 1934: Valerie Jane Morris-Goodall, known as Jane Goodall was born in London. She received a stuffed chimpanzee named Jubilliee that she still owns today.
  • 1941: Jane loved animals from a very early age. She reads a story of Dr.Doolittle which inspired her to go to Africa and study animals.
  • 1952: Jane went to secretarial scholl after high school. After graduating, she worked for her aunt. She then got a job at Oxford University where she typed documents. She later works for film studio.
  • 1956: She receives a letter that will change her life forever. Her friend invites her to visit their family in Kenya, Africa. She quits her job and get a job as waitress so she can save money.
  • 1957: Jane goes to Africa. While in Kenya she met famous anthropologist, Dr.Louis Leakey. She expresses her love for Africa and animals to him, he hires her to be his secretary at the Coryndon Museum in Nairobi. Dr.Leaky realizes that Jane is the best person to study chimps in the gombe Stream Reserve in tanzania. Jane heads back to England while Dr.Leaky finds funding for the project.
  • 1960: Jane and her mother, after finding a cook and stopping for supplies, set up camp at the Gombe Stream Reserve in Tanzania.
  • July 1960 - November 1960: Jane takes her notebook and binoculars and observe chimpanzees for hours. She takes notes of their daily habits and their way of finding clever solutions to problems. How they make simple tools when necessary. In November, Jane and her mother returns to England
  • 1961: She gets accepted by a chimp who she calls David greybeard. This opens up an opportunity for other chimps to accept her presence.
  • 1962: Dr.Leaky convinces Cambridge University to let her go in special program to receive her college degree. She graduates in 1966, becoming Dr.Jane Goodall.
  • 1962: She meets Baron Hugo van, a photographer/filmmaker from National Geographic who follows her around taking pictures while she works.
  • 1964: Jane marries Hugo. Jane gives birth to her son in March of 1967.
  • 1974: Jane and Hugo divorce in 1974. The same year she observes that chimp wage war on other groups of chimps and there she observes acts of cannibalism.
  • 1977: Jane starts the Jane Goodall Institute to help people understand the importance of preserving the forests and the animals who live in them. Over the years several programs have developed to help others realize that they, too, can make a difference in the world around them. Jane Goodall will always be known as a pioneer in the research of chimpanzees in the wild, and as a protector of the lands they live in.
